Ordered list

  1. Sholay (1975)

    2. Director: Ramesh Sippy

    Language: Hindi

    Description:

    A quintessential Bollywood masala film, "Sholay" is a blend of action, adventure, comedy,
    and drama. The story revolves around two criminals hired to capture a ruthless bandit,
    Gabbar Singh. The film is noted for its memorable characters, iconic dialogues, and epic narrative.

  2. Baahubali: The Beginning (2015)

    3. Director: S.S. Rajamouli

    Language: Telugu

    Description:

    This epic action film set in ancient India tells the story of two cousins vying for the
    throne of Mahishmati. With its grand scale, stunning visuals, and compelling storyline,
    "Baahubali: The Beginning" became a blockbuster and garnered international attention for Indian cinema.

  3. Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge (1995)

    4. Director: Aditya Chopra

    Language: Hindi

    Description:

    Often abbreviated as DDLJ, this romantic drama has become a cultural phenomenon in India
    It tells the love story of Raj and Simran, who meet during a trip to Europe but face family
    opposition upon returning to India. The film's charming performances and memorable music have made it a beloved classic.

  4. 3 Idiots (2009)

    5. Director: Rajkumar Hirani

    Language: Hindi

    Description:

    This comedy-drama tackles the pressures of the Indian education system through
    the lives of three engineering students. With its humorous yet thought-provoking narrative, "3 Idiots"
    has resonated with audiences and critics alike, becoming one of the highest-grossing Indian films of all time.

  5. Pather Panchali (1955)

    6. Director: Satyajit Ray

    Language: Bengali

    Description:

    This film, the first in Satyajit Ray's Apu Trilogy, is a cornerstone of Indian cinema
    It follows the life of a young boy, Apu, and his impoverished family in rural Bengal
    Known for its realistic portrayal of village life and its humanistic storytelling, "Pather
    Panchali" is celebrated worldwide for its cinematic brilliance.

Unordered-list

  • Hunza Valley

    • Description:

    Located in the Gilgit-Baltistan region, Hunza Valley is famous
    for its stunning landscapes, including the majestic Rakaposhi and Ultar Sar peaks. The
    valley offers breathtaking views, vibrant local culture, and opportunities for trekking and exploration.

  • Swat Valley

    • Description:

    Often referred to as the "Switzerland of Pakistan," Swat Valley
    in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa boasts lush green valleys, clear lakes, and snow-capped mountains. Mingora,
    Malam Jabba (a popular ski resort), and the historical Buddhist remains in Butkara are key attractions.

  • Skardu

    • Description:

    Located in Gilgit-Baltistan, Skardu serves as the gateway
    to some of the world's highest peaks, including K2. It features stunning landscapes such as Shangrila Resort,
    Deosai National Park, and the serene Sheosar Lake.

  • Mohenjo-Daro

    • Description:

    Located in the Sindh province, Mohenjo-Daro is one of the world's
    oldest cities, dating back to the Indus Valley Civilization. This archaeological site offers a fascinating
    insight into ancient urban planning and culture.
